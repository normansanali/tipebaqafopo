Who is Lamman Rucker?

Lamman Rucker is an American actor. He has played a role in daytime soap operas, As the World Turns and All My Children.

He has also done the movies like Why Did I Get Married? Why Did I Get Married Too?, and Meet the Browns.

Lamman Rucker: Birth Facts, Family, Childhood

Lamman Rucker was born on October 6, 1971, in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Rucker’s nationality is American and according to his ethnicity, he is African-American.

He is the son of Eric Rucker(father) and Malaya Rucker(mother). He has raised in Washington DC and Maryland area with his brother Chanu.

Lamman Rucker first had an interest in acting after he was placed in many child pageants. His first acting role was as Martin Luther King when he was in the 4th grade. He was in the drama club in 7th grade.

Education History

Lamman Rucker attended Duke Ellington School of the Arts in Washington, D.C, and completed his high school degree there. Later, for his further studies, he joined Duquesne University and Carnegie-Mellon University.

Lamman Rucker: Professional Life, Career

Lamman Rucker began to show his talent in the field of movies with the television film “The Temptations” in the role of Jimmy Ruffin in the year 1998. After working on the film, his luck did not favor him to work on any other projects for around five years.

Lamman had a tough time then. But fortunately, Lamman got an opportunity to work in the movie titled Revolution as Michael Suliman. Since then Lamman movies started to take a greater height. His next short film Redirect and Let’s Talk was released in the year 2005 and 2006 respectively.

In 2007, the short film The Commission, I’m Through with White Girls and Why Did I Get Married was released. Likewise from the year 2008 to 2010, every year Lamman acted in at least two movies. Which were Meet the Browns, Ball Don’t Lie, Words Unspoken, The Greatest Song, N-Secure and Why Did I Get Married Too?

Some of his recent movies are sugar Mommas, Where’s the Love, The Man in 3B, and so on. He later works in small movies and TV movies. Rucker also had a regular role opposite Mena Suvari in the short-lived WE TV drama series, South of Hell.

In 2015, he was cast as one of the leads in the Oprah Winfrey Network drama series, Greenleaf. He plays Jacob Greenleaf, the eldest son of Lynn Whitfield and Keith David’s characters.
