George Stephanopoulos Biography

George Robert Stephanopoulos is an American tv host, political commentator, and former Democratic advisor. He was born and brought up in Massachusetts, the U.S. Currently he is the chief anchor and political correspondent on ABC News as well as a coanchor with Robin Roberts on Good Morning America, and host of ABC’s Sunday morning current events news program, This Week. Moreover, he also serves as a regular fill-in anchor on ABC World News Tonight.

Earlier, before his career as a journalist, Stephanopoulos served as an advisor to the Democratic Party. He rose to become the communications director for the 1992 presidential campaign of Bill Clinton and subsequently became the communications director of the White House. He also served as a senior advisor for policy and strategy, prior to his departure in December 1996.

    George Stephanopoulos Politics

    Previously, before getting into journalism, Stephanopoulos was very much involved in politics. In 1988, he worked on the frontline in the Michael Dukakis 1988 U.S. presidential campaign. He also became the “floor man” for Dick Gephardt, U.S. House of Representatives Majority Leader.

    In 1992 he joined Clinton’s campaign team along with David Wilhelm and James Carville. he also got to serve as the de facto press secretary, briefing the press even though he wasn’t the official press secretary.

    From here he went on to begin his career in journalism, working as a political analyst for ABC News and serving as a correspondent on This Week, ABC’s Sunday morning public affairs program; World News Tonight, the evening news broadcast; Good Morning America, the morning news program; along with other various special broadcasts.

    George Stephanopoulos This Week

    Stephanopoulos became the host of This Week in September 2002. he rose ranks to be named   “Chief Washington Correspondent” in December 2005. The program’s title added the new host’s name.

    When named to the position, Stephanopoulos was a relative newcomer to the show, seizing longtime panelists and short-term co-hosts Sam Donaldson and Cokie Roberts who, for a few years, briefly replaced the longtime first host, David Brinkley.

    Fareed Zakaria was a weekly panelist in the first two years of Stephanopoulos’ tenure, expanding the show with his perspective on the world and Middle Eastern issues. He was the co-moderator of the debate between Mitt Romney, Ron Paul, and Rick Santorum on January 7, 2012.

    After Diane Sawyer’s departure from World News at the end of August 2014, Stephanopoulos was promoted to be the Chief Anchor at ABC News while retaining his current roles on GMA and This Week. Stephanopoulos now serves as the lead anchor for coverage of breaking news and major special events.
