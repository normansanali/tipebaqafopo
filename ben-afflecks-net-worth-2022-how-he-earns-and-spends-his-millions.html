A $600,000 cheque was shared between Affleck and Matt Damon for authoring “Good Will Hunting.”

Affleck and Damon didn’t receive a sizable salary, despite the fact that their 1997 film “Good Will Hunting,” for which they won an Oscar for best original screenplay, made them overnight stars in Hollywood.

In 2013, Affleck revealed to Boston Magazine that he and Damon shared the $600,000 from the sale of the script.

We were expected to receive $600,000, according to a Daily Variety article, he recalled. “Since we lacked credit, we utilised a copy of the Daily Variety to get the $3,000/monthly rental of this residence. “I don’t have credit, but this is who we are,” I said. And the landlord responded, “Fine, sure, all right.””

“We bought Jeep Cherokees for each other and rented fancier apartments because we believed that $600,000 would be enough to support us for 20 years. And within a year, we were bankrupt.”

For years, Affleck has been making an eight-figure salary.

Following “Good Will Hunting,” Affleck used films like “Armageddon,” “Pearl Harbor,” and “Daredevil” to launch his career as a huge Hollywood star.

Affleck has worked his way up to eight-figure salaries over the past few decades, including his roles as Batman in the DC Comics films “Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice” and “Justice League.”
