What is Tana Mongeau’s net worth?

Tana Mongeau, an American internet personality and rapper, has a net worth of $4 million. She rose to prominence through her YouTube channel, which boasts over 5 million subscribers.

Her “Storytime” videos are particularly popular, and she has since expanded her brand to include a music career and a show on MTV.

Tana Mongeau: Early Life and Career

Tana Mongeau was born on June 24, 1998, in Las Vegas, Nevada. She grew up in the city and established her self-titled YouTube channel in 2015. In due course, her channel gained immense popularity, and she amassed millions of viewers.

Tana Mongeau: A Controversial YouTube Star

Tana Mongeau is a YouTube star best known for her storytime videos. She courted controversy early on in her career with a video entitled “KICKED OUT OF WALMART” and has continued to thrive on it. In 2017, she was investigated by the FBI after someone hacked into her email and sent bomb threats.

Despite her controversial content, Mongeau has crossed over into other industries. In 2017, she released her debut single “Hefner” with an accompanying music video featuring Bella Thorne. She also appeared on the TV series Maury that same year. In 2018, she released two more singles: “W” and “F**k Up” and was featured on two songs on the album “God Hates Lil Phag.”

Mongeau is no stranger to controversy. In 2018, she hosted her own convention, TanaCon, which was a disaster and compared to Fyre Fest. She issued refunds and released a documentary about the event. Mongeau also starred in the MTV reality series “Tana Turns 21” and has appeared on several YouTube series.

Despite her success, Mongeau has struggled with demonetization on YouTube due to the controversial nature of her content. She has made up the difference by selling merchandise and creating sponsored content for brands that aren’t afraid of controversy, like Lounge Underwear.

Tana Mongeau’s Relationship History

Tana Mongeau was in a relationship with Bella Thorne from summer 2017 to February 2019. In spring 2019, she began dating fellow YouTuber Jake Paul and in June, announced their engagement. They were wed in July at Las Vegas’s Graffiti Mansion, but Mongeau later revealed that the ceremony was not official. She briefly changed her social media display names to “Tana Paul” but changed them back by the beginning of 2020.
