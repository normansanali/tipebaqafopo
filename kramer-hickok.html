Kramer Hickok is an American professional golfer who currently competes in the PGA Tour. He formerly played on the Web.com Tour and PGA Tour Canada. His major achievements include PGA Tour Canada's Player of the Year (2017), Dap Championship (2018), and Travelers Championship (2021). 

Hickock was born in Austin Texas on April 14, 1992. He is a former student of the University of Texas and was a freshman the same year as Jordan Speith also his roommate. In fact, he had caddied for Speith during 2011 AT&T Byron Nelson. Hickock graduated in 2015 with a degree in geography.  

Kramer Hickok's Career

Hickock turned professional in 2016 with his first tournament being the Panama Championship where he finished tied for 64th place. In 2017, he played on the PGA Tour Canada where he earned player of the year honors, winning twice during the season; the Players Cup and the Ontario Championship.

Kramer Hickok is the winner of the PGA Tour Canada Player of the Year 2017 SOURCE: Herald Sun

That season, he also won the Order of Merit. On the Web.com tour, he secured the 2018 DAP Championship ahead of Hunter Mahan and Matt Jones by three strokes. Going wire to wire, he matched the course record with a single round of 63 on the first day. He managed to place himself second in the 2018 edition of the Web.com tour, consequently earning his PGA Tour card for the 2019 season.

In his debut PGA season, Hickock made 13 cuts out of 24 competitions. In the next campaign, he was eligible for a total of seven payouts. His best finish in 2021 was at the Travelers Championship on June 24 where he ranked himself at 1st position with an overall score of 63-69-68-0 -- 200 (-10).

Kramer Hickock's Net Worth And Career Earnings

The following year, he made around $75,300 finishing 19th in the RBC Canadian Open. In 2018, his gross earnings totaled $396,969 from 17 out of 25 possible purses.
